About MaizeScan

A CNN-based maize leaf disease detection system — Graduation Project, ULK Data Science 2025/2026.

Background

Agriculture plays a crucial role in ensuring food security and economic development in Rwanda and across East Africa. Maize is the most widely produced staple crop, yet yield losses from leaf diseases — Northern Leaf Blight, Common Rust, and Gray Leaf Spot — can reach 30–80% in severe outbreak years.

MaizeScan was developed to provide farmers and extension officers with an accessible, AI-powered tool for rapid disease identification using a smartphone or tablet.

Model Architecture

  • Base: VGG16 pre-trained on ImageNet (13 conv layers + 3 FC layers)
  • Head: GlobalAvgPool → Dense(512, ReLU) → BN → Dropout(0.5) → Dense(256, ReLU) → BN → Dropout(0.3) → Softmax(4)
  • Phase 1: 30 epochs, frozen VGG16, LR = 1×10⁻⁴
  • Phase 2: 25 epochs, block5 unfrozen, LR = 1×10⁻⁵
  • Optimizer: Adam | Loss: Categorical Cross-Entropy

System Design (OOSADM)

The system was designed using Object-Oriented System Analysis and Design Methodology (OOSADM), producing four UML artefacts:

  • Use Case Diagram — Farmer and Administrator actors
  • Class Diagram — LeafImage, CNNModel, DiseaseClass, DiagnosisResult, FlaskApp
  • Sequence Diagram — 11-step leaf upload and diagnosis flow
  • Activity Diagram — Complete workflow with decision branches

Development followed an Agile Scrum-inspired approach across 4 sprints.
